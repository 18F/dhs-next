Solving Big Problems

Millions of Americans interact with government services every day. Veterans apply for benefits. Students compare financial aid opportunities. Small businesses seek loans. Too often, these interactions are made cumbersome by outdated tools and systems.

Our approach is simple: pair the country’s best public servants with top technology talent to improve how government works for the people. With headquarters in the Executive Office of the President and Digital Service teams networked throughout federal agencies, we can challenge the status quo.

Our Work

Vets.gov

With more than 1000 websites and 956 phone numbers, the VA customer experience is fragmented, frustrating, and confusing. Working with leaders at the VA, , we are building Vets.gov, a simple, easy to use site that provides veterans with useful information information that is clearly written and presented in one place. One website—not thousands. Vets.gov launched in November 2015 in beta, and is being put in front of Veterans as early as possible so we can see, as we build, what is working for them and what is not. As the site progresses, Veterans will be able to find and get more of what they need in one place.

Online Immigration System

For visa holders, a green card is essential and serves as a proof of identification and work authorization. Filing for a replacement green card can be an arduous process, involving lengthy paperwork and a six month wait. Partnering with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services, we launched an online platform to streamline the user experience. 300,000 individuals have filed the I-90 electronically since launch, saving an average of 20 minutes per person, earning the new system a 93% satisfaction rate.

College Scorecard

For students, selecting a college may be the single most important investment of their lives, but finding clear, reliable data on critical questions is a challenge. We launched College Scorecard to empower students with the most comprehensive, reliable data ever published on employment outcomes and success in repaying student loans. 1.4+ million students have benefited from these data, and the College Scorecard has been accessed over 9.4 million times in less than 6 months.
